Structures and Fire

Structural Fire Engineering is a multidisciplinary field integrating fire science, structural mechanics, and performance-based safety engineering for buildings and critical infrastructure. Fire is one of the most severe hazards a structure can experience, and its effects are amplified by other extreme events such as earthquakes, blasts, and progressive collapse scenarios.

My consulting and research activities focus on structural fire performance, wildfire–structure interaction, and resilience of buildings and infrastructure systems. My work includes advanced analysis of steel and composite structures in fire, post-fire stability of tall buildings, and community-scale wildfire risk modeling. I also develop computational tools for transient heat transfer and fire–structure interaction, most notably the FEHEAT finite element platform.
